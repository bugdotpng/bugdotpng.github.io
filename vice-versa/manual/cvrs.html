What is CVRS?

CVRS is the Center for Vice Research and Support. Our goal is to mitigate Vice-related crises and continue expanding research in the field of Vicology. We provide Vice management services and support and are available 24/7.

Wards

Each CVRS station is separated into two distinct wards. The Passive Ward is open to the public and accepts walk-ins, but patients will need to be examined by the lead Vice Management Specialist before treatment can begin. The Active Ward—and more specifically, the Urgent Care team—also requires referal from a medical professional, including specialists from the Passive Ward.

Passive
Passive

Primarily concerned with providing long-term care while aggregating data to send to the Research Sector and Active Ward.

Each Passive Ward consists of at least one:

  • Intake Coordinator
  • Vice Management Specialist
  • Branch Recovery Specialist
  • Forecast Specialist
  • Report Coordinator
  • Behavioral Analyst
  • Blight Analyst

Most agents in the Passive Ward are not trained to engage in Branches, though Intake Coordinators and Vice Management Specialists are able to Suspend Branches if a rogue Branch occurs before the Host is admitted to the Active Ward.

Active
Active

Predominantly focused on mitigating rogue Branches by acting on the research provided by the Passive Ward.

Each Active Ward consists of two teams:

Internal

The team holding down the fort, taking public walk-ins and admissions from the Passive Ward. Urgent Care is deployed by the order of the Floor Manager on staff.

  • Urgent Care
    • Branch Specialist
    • Triage Specialist
    • Vice Analyst
  • Intake Coordinator
  • Floor Manager
External

The boots-on-the-ground team deployed out into the public, on call to respond to rogue or underprepared Branches 24/7. Urgent Care is deployed by the order of the Target Hunter on duty.

  • Urgent Care
    • Branch Specialist
    • Triage Specialist
    • Vice Analyst
  • Target Hunter
  • Branch Analyst
  • Optional: Dispersion Analyst

Sectors

CVRS is divided into three sectors: Public Safety, Public Health, and Research. Each sector is tasked with unique roles, but often collaborate with other sectors to aggregate or share data.

Each Sector contains agents from both the Passive and Active wards to ensure an efficient and holistic approach to care and research. Urgent Care teams have representation from each Sector to ensure that the most thoughtful and informed actions can be taken to serve our community.

Some agents from the Research Sector are deployed as Pseudo Adjuncts with a role to simply observe and collect data. While they are technically considered part of the Urgent Care team, Pseudo Adjuncts are not trained to engage in Branches, nor is it biologically possible to do so.* Learn more in The Role of Adjuncts.

* As is understood by our current set of data, 2023.

Public Safety

The Public Safety sector is primarily concerned with public relations, and are the public-facing, communications team of CVRS. Public Safety agents are specially trained in social and behavioral sciences and are either actively or passively aggregating data, depending on an agent's focus.

Forecast Specialist
Passive
Studies changes in Vice behavior and presents findings to Target Hunters and Branch Specialists; often partners with Branch and Behavioral analysts from the Research sector for peer review
  • Equipment: Docs
  • Branch Training: Basic
  • Patch: Optional
Report Coordinator
Passive
Receives and records Branch reports submitted by Target Hunters, Branch Specialists, Branch Analysts, and Intake Coordinators for the Forecast Specialist to analyze; manages and organizes the data bank, whereas the Forecast Specialist draws patterns and makes predictions based on the data
  • Equipment: Docs
  • Branch Training: Basic
  • Patch: Optional
Target Hunter
Active
Plain-clothes agents that patrol high-traffic areas or hot spots, as indicated by a Forecast Specialist; specially trained to recognize and prevent Branches prior to initiation; trained to Suspend if a Branch Specialist is not available
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Data Interchange Manager, Push Manager
  • Branch Training: Suspend
  • Patch: Recommended
Branch Specialist
Active Urgent Care
Emergency responders for initiated, often out of control Branches; specially trained to Commit Branches, but will Suspend if necessary for public safety; Branch Specialists are the mobile equivalent of Floor Managers, having a ton of industry knowledge
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Data Interchange Manager
  • Branch Training: Resolve
  • Patch: Required

Public Health

The Public Health sector is broad in its skillset, though it focuses mostly on administering medical care, treatment, and support to Hosts, including pharmacological and therapeutic treatments. Public Health agents are well-versed in the physiological effects of Vices on Hosts and the appropriate treatments to administer.

Floor Manager
Active
Oversees the intake bay and is the first responder trained to Commit any rogue Branches; Floor Managers are information banks ready for anything from a generic Branch to incredibly niche extremes; they have a strong relationship with Branch and Behavioral Analysts
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Data Interchange Manager, Push Manager
  • Branch Training: Commit
  • Patch: Required
Triage Specialist
ActiveUrgent Care
Performs physicals and reviews intake reports to quickly determine treatment for the Branch Specialist; trained to Suspend in the event of a rogue Branch or lack of Branch Specialist
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Data Interchange Manager
  • Branch Training: Suspend
  • Patch: Recommended
Intake Coordinator
Active
An extremely skilled public relations agent that is the face of either the Passive or Active ward; both Intake Coordinators handle media, while Passive Intake Coordinators handle public admissions, and Active Intake Coordinators handle rouge Branches and Urgent Care requests; Intake Coordinators are extremely observant and provide the first wave of data collected from the Host
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Data Interchange Manager
  • Branch Training: Suspend
  • Patch: Recommended
Vice Management Specialist
Passive
Provides long-term care and treatment plans for Vice Management; works predominantly with Hosts who forego Rescission, but can also guide a Host through the process of Rescission alongside a Branch Specialist and Vice Analyst, if desired
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Push Manager
  • Branch Training: Resolve
  • Patch: Required
Branch Recovery Specialist
Passive
Predominantly works with Hosts who have recently experienced a difficult Branch or had adverse effects after a Commit or Suspension; they often partner with Blight Analysts to record any anomalies and provide care plans; will hand a Host off to a Vice Management Specialist if they’re not responding to treatment or appear to have chronic symptoms
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles
  • Branch Training: Extend
  • Patch: Optional

Research

The Research sector compiles and studies the data gathered by active agents. Research agents collaborate closely with Forecast Specialists, Report Coordinators, and Intake Coordinators to review, compile, and parse data.

Vice Analyst
ActiveUrgent Care
Deployed to active Branches to observe and aggregate data about Vices and their respective Branches; Vice Analysts focus on high-level, big-picture data and are often looking for both trends and anomalies, but are trained to Commit or Suspend Branches as a last resort. Many Vice Analysts go on to specialize in specific analyses, such as Branches, Vice behavior, Blights, and Dispersion. While not incredibly skilled in any specific area, Vice Analysts are the only agents in the Research Sector to be deployed as actively involved Adjuncts, rather than Pseudo Adjuncts.
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Data Interchange Manager
  • Branch Training: Commit
  • Patch: Required
Branch Analyst
ActivePseudo
Focuses predominantly on the science of Branches and the physiological effects of Branches on the mind and body of both Hosts and Adjuncts. Branch Analysts are often deployed with the Urgent Care team as a Pseudo Adjunct and very rarely aid in Commits.
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Docs
  • Branch Training: Suspend
  • Patch: Optional
Behavioral Analyst
Passive
Focuses on Vice Behaviors as they build up to and recover from Branches; Behavioral Analysts work alongside Vice Management Specialists to follow Hosts through treatment plans, track progress, and report anomalies or successful treatments.
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Docs
  • Branch Training: Extend
  • Patch: Optional
Blight Analyst
Passive
A fairly niche field focused exclusively on Blight manifestation and the effects prolonged Blights can have on a Host's body; while many are focused on furthering research, Blight Analysts are also qualified to prescribe Suppressants and treatment plans under the guidance of a Vice Management Specialist.
  • Equipment: Docs
  • Branch Training: Extend
  • Patch: Optional
Dispersion Analyst
ActivePseudo
An incredibly niche and understaffed field focused exclusively on the science of Dispersion, though they are often unable to assist in preventing Dispersion, as they are only involved as a Pseudo Adjunct; while uncommon, the presence of a Dispersion Analyst at a Branch is widely regarded as a death sentence. Only a handful of Hosts have managed a successful Resolution despite the presence of a Dispersion Analyst.
  • Equipment: Parsing Goggles, Docs
  • Branch Training: Extend
  • Patch: Optional

Branch Training

Many CVRS agents are required to receive varying levels of Branch Training prior to their official start date. The level of training coincides with the agent's role.

Training LevelBasic

4 Weeks

Basic Training is mandatory for all CVRS agents and gives a high-level overview of Vice anatomy, physiology, and psychology. During this time, agents learn about the properties of Branches.

The following roles are available as career options after Basic Training has been completed:

  • Forecast Specialist
  • Report Coordinator

Training LevelExtend

6 Weeks

Building on the knowledge acquired in Basic Training, agents learn how to stall Branches until help arrives. Agents must past a written exam in order to become certified.

The following roles require Extension Training:

  • Behavioral Analyst
  • Blight Analyst
  • Branch Recovery Specialist
  • Dispersion Analyst

Training LevelSuspend

12 Weeks

Suspension Training is the first level of training where Agents learn from Active Hosts and are brought in as Pseudo Adjuncts for training purposes. The Agent must successfully Suspend a Branch in order to receive certification.

The following roles require Suspension Training:

  • Target Hunter
  • Triage Specialist
  • Intake Coordinator
  • Branch Analyst

Training LevelCommit

16 Weeks

Rather than bringing in agents as Pseudo Adjuncts only, agents are often brought in to aid in live Branches, focusing on seeing a Commit through. Agents are trained to Suspend if Committing isn't an option. Agents receive certification after a successful Commit as the Primary Adjunct.

The following roles require Commit Training:

  • Vice Analyst
  • Floor Manager

Training LevelResolve

24 Weeks

Resolution Training is the most rigorous Branch Training and requires agents to routinely Commit Branches. Many agents with this degree of experience do not Suspend Branches unless a Commit would be threatening the safety of the Host or Adjuncts. An agent must successfully Resolve a Vice in order to become certified.

The following roles require Resolution Training:

  • Vice Management Specialist
  • Branch Specialist

Equipment

Upon completion of training, agents are provided with an equipment kit to aid in their varying roles. Below is a list of equipment provided by CVRS, who they are given to, and what they are used for.

Notice

Equipment provided by CVRS must only be used by certified CVRS Agents, and improper use may be punishable by up to $5000 in fines.

Docs
A digitized, up-to-date copy of the latest DMV3. Copies are automatically updated to reflect the most recent research findings. New Docs will be issued upon a new version. Docs can act as a rudimentary DIM, allowing agent observations to be submitted for review. Docs are provided to:
  • Forecast Specialists
  • Report Coordinators
  • Branch Analysts
  • Behavioral Analysts
  • Blight Analysts
  • Dispersion Analysts
Parsing Goggles
Polarized goggles that help agents observe the status of Active Hosts by making the Ionic Tunnels visible, known for their trademark orange tint. Styles can vary between locations, but most Passive agents are given the safety goggles style, while Active agents have goggles closer in appearance to aviation eyeware. Note: Some Vices have unique characteristics that help the tunnels avoid detection. Parsing Goggles are provided to:
  • Target Hunters
  • Branch Specialists
  • Floor Managers
  • Triage Specialists
  • Intake Coordinators
  • Vice Management Specialists
  • Branch Recovery Specialists
  • Vice Analysts
  • Branch Analysts
  • Behavioral Analysts
  • Dispersion Analysts
Data Interchange Manager
A communication tool used primarily between Active agents, similar in appearance to a small tablet. This tool allows agents to communicate with each other, request backup, and log findings, even during active Branches. Data Interchange Managers are provided to:
  • Target Hunters
  • Branch Specialists
  • Floor Managers
  • Triage Specialists
  • Intake Coordinators
  • Vice Analysts
Push Manager
A single-use device that allows an Agent to force their way into an active Branch as an Adjunct, with a success rate of about 72%. Push Managers are similar in appearance to tasers and attract and rekindle any stray Ionic Tunnels to provide a path to enter the active Branch. Often used in conjunction with Parsing Goggles. Forcing onself into a Branch can be painful for the agent, Host or any Adjuncts involved if Push Managers are used too often or incorrectly. If used outside of an active Branch, Push Managers have been known to initiate Branches. After certification, agents are provided a set of six and advised to use sparingly. Request for new kits are subject to denial if the supervisor suspects abuse. Push Managers are provided to:
  • Target Hunters
  • Floor Managers
  • Vice Management Specialists

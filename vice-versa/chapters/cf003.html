Vice Versa Danny Suárez • Case File: 003

❯ ❯❯
Content Warning

This chapter contains discussion of suicide and medical negligence, as well as depictions of assisted suicide, self harm, vomiting, and body horror.

Genesis Schwarzschild had never been popular, but that wasn’t something she lost sleep over. She had more important things to do, like color in her fingernails with black sharpie and make kandi bracelets for her best friend, Danny Suárez.

The two met in middle school, after having been paired up to work on a science project together. Once they realized the other was “cool” and had “good music taste”, they each dropped their preppy facades and were able to enjoy the catharsis of being their true, edgy selves together without fear of judgment.

This was fulfilling when they hung out at the mall or had movie nights, but at school, their alternative tendencies began to ostracize them. Gen was quickly labeled as a “weird goth chick” and Danny, a “depressed emo freak”, but that didn’t get to either of them, so long as they had each other. A pact was made once they started high school, that they would be friends forever, no matter what.

“You’ve been acting weird lately,” Gen remarked as she picked through her CD collection. “Well, you’re always weird, but you know what I mean. Weirder than usual.”

Danny turned a page in the manga he was holding above his head. He was sprawled across Gen’s bed, his gangly limbs draped over the sides. “Oh, yeah?”

“Yeah, man. You fell asleep in study hall the other day instead of passing notes with me like you always do, and then you bailed on movie night. I couldn’t find you at lunch today, either. You never miss crispitos day. I honestly didn’t even expect you to want to hang out today.”

“Yeah, I definitely thought about just going straight home.”

“Why?”

He turned another page with a bored sniff. “Doctor says I’m depressed.”

“Well, yeah. We know that. But why wouldn’t you want to hang out with me?”

“I dunno. I just haven’t been feeling it lately.”

“Feeling what?”

He dropped the manga on his chest and let his hands fall to the bed with a huff. “It. All of it. Not a fan.”

“What, like being alive?”

“Eh, kinda. I’m just over it.”

“Are you still mad about the patch thing?”

He quickly scrambled upright. “What? No!”

“Because you’ve been off ever since I told you. I said I’m sorry I didn’t tell you sooner, but it’s because I knew you would sulk like this. I don’t need you to congratulate me or anything, but you could at least pull a few sticks outta your ass.”

“Oh, come on, Gen! I don’t care about that at all. I’ve made my peace with the fact I’ll probably never get my stupid patch. God, you can be so self-centered sometimes. It’s not always about you.”

“You know, my therapist says I’m very mature for not beating the shit out of you all the time like I want to.”

“Oh, so you talk to your therapist about me?”

“I talk to my therapist about everyone! That’s the point! And if you’re not sulking about the stupid patch, then what the hell is going on?”

“Well, I was fucking fine until you accused me of being jealous!”

“I didn’t say you were jealous. I said you were sulking I didn’t tell you sooner.”

“Well, either way, I’m not. I’m not anything. If I’m anything, it’s fucking tired! Of everything! I’m just tired, okay? I need a break from it all!”

“Don’t we all! I still don’t think that’s an excuse to avoid your best friend.”

“Well, pardon me for not thinking about your needs when I’m having an episode!”

“See! I knew something was up!”

“Nothing is up, Gen. Like I said, I’m just tired.”

“Of what?”

He shook his fists in frustration and let out a grunt. “Gah! All of it! Especially this! What we’re doing now! I feel like I’m being interrogated! Can’t a guy just sulk every once in a while?”

“Well, yeah, if you woulda given me a heads up! I thought I did something wrong!”

“It’s not about you, Gen!”

She pulled another manga from the shelf and chucked it at him. “What are you so tired for anyway? And you don’t seem tired, you seem like you’re tweakin’ out.”

“Does it really matter why?”

“Yes, because we’re friends! And friends talk to each other about their problems instead of bottling it all up inside until you start doing dumb shit like this!”

“I was perfectly fine until you came at me sideways. F-Y-I.”

“You most certainly were not. F-Y-I. Even Rami noticed you’ve been acting weird.”

“Well, tell Rami to mind his business.”

“Why are you being such a bitch to people who care about you?”

“Because I just want to be left alone! I’m sick of pretending like there isn’t a fucking parasite writhing around in my body!”

She sat back with a smug grin. “Okay, now we’re getting somewhere.”

“We most certainly are not getting anywhere. It’s as simple as that! I’m just tired of putting on a brave face.”

“What do you mean a ‘brave face’? You haven’t even had a Branch since you were ten.”

“So? Doesn’t mean I’m not tortured by this stupid thing every damn day!”

“I don’t think anyone’s expecting you to put on a brave face, Danny. Lots of people are dealing with the same shit you are, so I don’t think anyone’s gonna hold it against you if you sulk around more than you already do.”

“I resent that, you know.”

“What, that you’re always sulking?”

“Well, yes, because I’m not, but I resent even more that people are dealing with the same shit I am. That’s just not true! Mine is so much…weirder than everyone else’s.”

“It’s not really a competition.”

“Yeah, it’s not literally a competition, but it certainly feels like one! It’s so embarrassing that I don’t even have a patch yet. Everyone else keeps having Branches and they get to learn all this stuff about their Vices, and here I am, patchless and stupid and not knowing anything about my Vice because it’s not acting like it’s supposed to! It makes me feel like a child!”

“Oh my god, it doesn’t matter! Other people’s Branch patterns have nothing to do with anything. And just because someone’s having Branches all the time doesn’t mean they’re learning anything! Stop comparing yourself to everyone. It doesn’t matter!”

“Easy for you to say when you’ve got the coolest patch ever. Trying to tell me it doesn’t matter when you literally have a scythe!”

“Who cares how cool someone’s patch is?”

“Everyone! There’s literally a tier list going around! And you know where I’m at? Not even on the list because I don’t have one!”

“Oh my god, I don’t know how to say it any clearer: it doesn’t matter!”

“Well, maybe it matters to me! Maybe I’m self conscious my stupid, horrible body refuses to cooperate and help me out! It makes me feel like I’m doing something wrong! I feel like my body and I aren’t even on the same team! It’s got a mind of its own!”

“I don’t know why you think you’d get a patch if you’re not having Branches. Your body only makes a patch when it thinks you need it.”

He dragged his hands down his face, smearing his eyeliner as he exhaled an exasperated groan. “Can you turn that part of you off for five seconds? Why do you always have an answer for everything?”

“Because I know the answer to everything you’re asking!”

“I’m not asking questions, I’m just venting! If you would stop looking at me like I’m a problem to solve, you’d fucking realize that!”

“Would you just tell me what’s really going on? You talk about other people like this all the time, but you’ve never talked to me like this. Have you always felt this way about me?”

He leapt off the bed, gesticulating passive aggressively. “I don’t know how to be any clearer, Gen. This isn’t about you!”

She stepped toward him, throwing her hands out. “Then what is it about? I’m worried about you, Danny!”

“Oh, great! Now I’m the bad guy for making you worried!”

“Seriously? That’s not what I’m saying! If you want to rant so bad, then rant! And I’m going to listen, because I care about you! So get that through your thick skull, and let’s fucking hear it! Rant away!”

He puffed out his chest, albeit somewhat hesitantly. “Okay, fine! I will! I live in constant fear that this fucking parasite inside of me is like a bomb that can go off at any moment! Everyone I’ve ever met has some formula they can avoid, but I’m terrified to do anything because I don’t have any idea what this thing’s fucking deal is! Anyone else who got their Vice at the same age I did has had, what, 10 Branches by now? I’ve had two and there were no similarities between them. I couldn’t even try to find patterns because there weren’t any! Do you know how terrifying that is?

“They said the second one was a fluke. They said they got rid of it, but I swear I can feel it still wiggling around in there. They say all of this is just residual blights from my rescinded Phi Vice, but I see it every time I vomit, Gen. I have to stare at it in the sink or in the toilet or on the floor and act like throwing up a wad of sludge the size of my head is normal. Because the only people in my life who said they could help me believe that it doesn’t exist and I’m just being dramatic.

“Do you know how many AV Shots I’ve had? I couldn’t even tell you because I’ve lost count. I should be fucking immune to Vices with how much shit they’ve stabbed me with, but it hasn’t done a thing. I still have to leave class to vomit my guts out in the bathroom and then get made fun of for having to go to the bathroom all the time. Everyone thinks I shit like five times a day! You know I barely passed freshman year, right? I had too many absences and missed too many tests from all this bullshit. I have to listen to half of everyone around me tell me I’m dysfunctional and broken, and the other half tell me I’m over-exaggerating and dramatic. And no one’s stopped to think I’m somewhere in the middle. I’m fucked up and I’m explicitly telling people what I’m dealing with, but no one’s fucking listening to me!”

Black, pointed horns protruded from the sides of his skull. They were slick, shimmering like a thick, black oil. His pupils had flattened out, resembling those of a bull’s, and his fingers had sharpened to a jagged point, looking as if they were dipped in ink.

Gen took a step back. “I-I don’t think these are your usual blights, Danny.”

“Don’t tell me what is and isn’t usual for me! You have no fucking idea how many times I’ve thrown up just today!”

“No! I mean right now! Look at yourself!”

Danny stumbled over to her mirror, gripping the sides with his claws. His face went white as his stomach dropped, and his hands began to tremble. He slowly shook his head, then faster as he backed up, nearly tripping over himself. “O-Oh my god. Is this a sick fucking joke? Are you serious? I can’t do this again. It’s the same as last time. I can’t fucking do this again. I’m not ready, Gen!”

“It’s okay, it’s okay! I’m here to help you this time!” Her hands, too, were shaking, as she reached to console him.

He slapped her hands away. “You can’t fix this, Gen! I don’t care how good you think you are; this isn’t something that can be fixed! CVRS couldn’t even fix it!” He fell to his knees, gripping his head in agony. “Why won’t my stupid fucking body help me out? Why isn’t it giving me a patch? I need it, Gen. I need a patch or I can’t get out of this!”

His claws dug into his skull, breaking the skin. A thin stream of blood trickled down his temples. “Oh my god, I’m going to get dispersed. There’s no way I can commit this without a patch, Gen! You have to get out of here!”

“Are you kidding? No fucking way!” She collapsed to the floor and draped her arms around him. “I’ll never leave you. You’re not doing this alone!”

He struggled against her grip, but she tightened her arms around him. He sobbed, oil brimming and spilling down his cheeks. A tacky sludge bubbled in his throat, and he felt like he was drowning. He lunged forward with a gag, spilling black tar down Gen’s back. “Please, for the love of god. I don’t want my last minutes to be spent wondering if I’m going to kill you, too! Just let me die alone!”

She shook her head, tears carrying mascara down her face. It was near impossible to ignore the sound of splitting flesh as his horns stretched from his temples.

He sobbed into her shoulder, tears and spit and sludge soaking her shirt. For a minute, the two remained in their embrace. Gen rubbed his back as her mind reeled for a solution. She noticed she hadn’t felt the magnetic pull of a staging radius. She wondered if it was a flareup, a false positive, or just the erratic, unpredictable behavior of a bizarre Vice that neither of them understood.

With a spasm, he heaved a sticky clot of tar down her back. He writhed desperately, breaking free of her grasp as he shakily rose to his feet. He was having trouble catching his breath as the sludge stuck to his throat. “No. No, Gen. Get back! Something’s wr—”

He threw his head back, and the sound of cracking bones and squelching organs pounded against Gen’s eardrums. A thin, red line ran across his stomach, blood oozing and beading along the incision. His skin began to part, stretching the freshly-formed clots into thin strings of dark red.

It was a nauseating, burning pain so deep in his core that he struggled to maintain consciousness. It brought tears to his eyes and drained the color from his face. It was a pain so intense, he could feel its presence permanently etching itself into his bones. A pain so extreme, that it caused all the other muscles in his body to tense and tie themselves into knots.

The incision snapped open and his upper body hinged backwards as he shrieked in pain. Rows of jagged teeth sprouted from his flesh along the opening as sludge and entrails cascaded from the gaping maw. He dragged his claws down his face, splitting his skin as he wailed.

A cold sweat broke out across Gen’s forehead as she stumbled backward, unable to peel her eyes from Danny’s convulsing body. He had told her about his Branch at 10 years old, but no amount of detail could have prepared her to witness a gaping maw splitting her friend in half.

A magnetic pulse radiated from the soles of his feet, sending a wave of disorienting nausea over Gen. She clambered upright, her knees nearly giving out, and she swallowed hard.

“Danny. It’s starting. Let me help.” Her voice shook as she struggled to ward off the nausea.

He took a step back, sobbing. “No! Don’t come near me! Don’t fucking touch me!” The back of his knees hit the bed and he fell backwards. He scrambled further away from her, throwing every pillow and stuffed animal at her.

“Stop it! Let me do this!”

“It’s a suicide mission, Gen! You can’t! I’m not going to let you sacrifice yourself for me. Just let me die in peace!”

Her hand trembled as she held it out to the side. A bright, cold light emanated from her palm, and a long rod of light extended to the floor. The top extended into a long, curved blade, slowly and hesitantly. Its form was unstable, almost fluid in her grasp.

Her voice steadied. “I’m going to fix this whether you like it or not. I’m not giving up on you.”

“Are you insane? Everyone else has! Look at me! I’m a lost cause!” He doubled over with a wince, wrapping his arms around his stomach. The teeth snagged his skin, and sludge oozed between his fingers.

His shoulders shook as he wept. He tightened his grip around his stomach, pressing the teeth deeper into his flesh.

“Even if you could fix it,” he stammered out between sobs, “I-I don’t think I want you to. I can’t keep doing this. I don’t want to have to face this for the rest of my life. It’s only getting worse. I don’t want to do this anymore. I don’t want to be here anymore.”

“Stop it!” Gen cried. “Don’t fucking say that! We’ll figure it out! And I’ll be by your side every step of the way! When we made our pact, I meant every word I said. We’re best friends forever, no matter what!”

He had doubled over, his hair shrouding his face. “We can still be best friends even if I’m not here.”

She smeared the makeup-filled tears across her face. “Stop saying that. I’m going to help you!”

He was mostly quiet, occasionally whimpering in pain.

Gen stomped her foot as she cried harder. “Fucking talk to me, Danny!”

“If you really want to help…” he began softly, “you’ll let me go out on my own terms.”

He lifted his face to her. She had never seen him look so certain. Even among his spilled entrails, his open torso, his bleeding scalp, he was almost smiling. An uncharacteristic calmness had washed over him as the teeth bore into his flesh. Truly, in all their years together, she had never seen him look so certain. It was all she ever wanted for him, to feel relief. The idea that his suffering would end at her hand was tantalizing. Even if it wasn’t in the Branch Domain, she could still help.

He calmly got on his knees before her, holding his balled fists in his lap as blood streamed down his arms and stained his jeans. He meekly lowered his head, then looked up at her to see her scythe readied, pulled back behind her. Its form was solid, it shone a brilliant cyan, and her hand was steady.

“Thank you.”

Regret tore through her body as her scythe fazed through his neck. It was a clean cut, confident and sharp, but regret came too late, and she fell to the floor in agony as his head slipped from his neck. Her scythe lost its form and came raining down in a dazzling mist, showering them in a luminous blanket of cyan as she tightly embraced his headless body.

“No, no, no! Why did you let me do that?” she cried, digging her nails into his back. “I should’ve died with you. Don’t leave me here alone!”

The emotions crawling up her throat were too intense for her to handle. Her head felt heavy and her vision blurred as she crawled over to vomit profusely into the trash can. She vomited until all that came out was a tacky, yellow bile, and her neck ached and spasmed with fatigue.

She weakly crawled back to him and collapsed on his motionless chest. Nothing could have prepared her for lying next to her lifeless friend, who died at her own hands. An act so heinous and unforgivable, she never could have imagined it. A mercy kill executed in a time of weakness and fear, immediately regretted and chastised as a self-serving act. She sliced through her own wrists as penance, tearing through her skin in long, deep gashes as the teeth had torn through his.

Her brain was a reeling mess of static, a dull headache growing stronger, pressing harder against her temples. She laid her head on Danny’s chest and gripped his hand in hers, giving the back of his hand a gentle kiss. Though he only laid lifeless for an hour, for that full hour, Gen grieved her best friend.

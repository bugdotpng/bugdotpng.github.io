1.04: Door //////

A young child fidgeted restlessly in a creaky, wooden chair, alone in the familiar tiled room. He had a timid posture, his head lowered and hands placed neatly in his lap. Wavy, shoulder-length hair shrouded his face and cascaded down his cheeks in loose, brunette spirals. The fluorescent lights relentlessly bore down from above, urging beads of sweat across his anxious brow as his heart fluttered in anticipation.

The scene had been relentlessly looping in the back of Danny's head all morning. It wasn't particularly surprising that he was having these dreams again, considering yesterday's events. Vices seemed to be an unavoidable topic at the moment, no matter how hard he tried to avoid them, and these dreams—or memories, rather—tended to resurface when too much attention was brought to the parasite writhing inside of him. Sure, it was Emory who actually had the Branch, but it reminded him that they truly were at the mercy of their sentient tumors. The hair on the back of his neck had been standing on-end since yesterday.

"Danny?" Rami jarred him from his trance as he thumbed through an intimidatingly thick stack of papers. "I could really use your cooperation here. My shift starts soon."

He muttered an apology as he shifted his gangly body in the awkwardly-shaped office chair.

They were meeting in what appeared to be a generic CVRS administrative area, any and all surfaces devoid of character. The already small office was crammed with two bulky chairs and an unnecessarily large, wooden desk that Rami also struggled to get comfortable behind. His coordinated planner decorated with puffy stickers and rainbow tabs brought some much-needed color into the room, and the tight quarters allowed for his warm, bergamot fragrance to overpower the trademark CVRS stench of rubbing alcohol. Rami's presence had done a good job of making the circumstances almost tolerable, had they been doing literally anything other than talking about Vices.

"So, can you do that?" Rami continued shuffling papers around, pausing periodically to quickly scribble out a signature.

"Can I do what?"

"Fill this out and hand to Kieran at the front desk before you leave. But before that..." He pushed the stack of papers toward him, then patted around the various pockets of his lab coat. "...we've gotta do an oral interview."

Rami didn't have to look up to know Danny's face had fallen into a scowl.

"Don't look at me like that. It's just protocol. Don't take any of this personally."

"Hard not to," he muttered.

"Listen, I'm on your side, man. Gah, where's the stupid..." His coat-searching grew more frantic the more empty pockets he came across. "I mean, I was the one who insisted on conducting the interview instead of a Branch Recovery Specialist, as is standard protocol. BRS agents are notoriously thorough and probably would've had you trapped here for hours. Aha!" He whipped out a dated tape recorder and placed it squarely between them, gently nudging it to the perfect center. "I'm doing this off the clock, too, mind you. Figured this way would be easier on you."

"No part of this is easy."

"Easy-er. Trying my best here."

For the first time that morning, he had finally made eye contact with Danny. He seemed frazzled, twitchy, even more than usual. There was a tightness in the corner of his mouth that alluded to a negative emotion Danny couldn't quite put his finger on. Irritation, perhaps?

Danny knew something was off when Rami got straight to business without so much as a "Morning! How are you doing?" or any other greeting he had in his arsenal of social niceties. It wasn't like he necessarily wanted to expound on his current headspace, but the gesture would've at least been appreciated.

The orange-tinted lenses of Rami's small, round glasses were awash with the fluorescent lighting overhead, sending a beam of light back at Danny that gave the allusion he was under a spotlight. He couldn't help but feel like his every move was being studied behind those trademark amber lenses.

His chest tightened. "Why can't you just fudge the responses and let me out of here? I'm not gonna be all that much help anyway."

"Well, this may come as a surprise to you, Danny," Rami sighed as he tented his fingers with a thin, forced smile. "I care about Emory's well-being and would like to know if there's anything out of sorts with this Branch." He hovered his hand over the tape recorder. "Sooner we get started, the sooner I can get you out of here."

He stood in the middle of the hallway, squinting at the lengthy paragraph he was holding a few inches from his face. The packet was chock-full of disclaimers, fluff, and industry jargon, and despite his familiarity with the CVRS lexicon, he struggled to parse exactly what the legalese and even Rami were saying. It didn't help that the rubbing alcohol assaulting his nostrils was making it difficult for him to think. Waves of nausea made sweat bloom across his brow.

With his gaze fixed on the incomprehensible mess of words, the walls around him began to close in on him, his breaths became shallow, and a growing, external pressure began to push against his temples. He whipped his head up with a gasp, the sudden movement throwing off his balance, and he braced himself against the wall.

Or rather, it was a window, textured glass blocks stacked into a towering grid that spanned the height of the wall and stretched down the hallway. At the end of the window, there was a large, metal door with a digital sign above it. His stomach dropped, and he suddenly realized why he had felt so restless all morning. CVRS was enough to put him on edge, sure, but the familiar sight of the glowing red sign that read "In Session" made him realize he had been here before.

Albeit, he had been in Primary Care, but the distorted figures shuffling behind the warped glass elicited the same unease that he had felt back when he was a child. He had been on the other side of the glass, positioned squarely in the center of the room, all eyes on him.

He scrambled to distract himself with the paperwork, knowing full well if he indulged his memories any further, things would quickly spiral out of control. He bolted down the hall, quickly initialing each page without reading Rami's accounts. Rami was an observant person, he rationalized, certainly more observant than him. There was no sense in double-checking his work; he was Emory's primary Adjunct, after all.

You'll be done in less than 10 minutes, Daniel, a staticky voice echoed in the back of his head. We'll take good care of you.

His heart pounded against his ribcage; all he could hear was the deafening sound of his blood rushing through his veins. He picked up the pace, now power-walking down the corridor, sweat trickling down his tensed jaw. He scribbled his last initial, skipped over the section to add any additional observations, and stared at the final page. It was short, but had open-ended questions, each number highlighted. He grit his teeth and offered a few terse sentence fragments.

The large, metal door loomed behind him, its presence heavy and unwavering no matter how far back it was. His shoulders were stiff and his ears rang with a high-pitched squeal. Pinpricks of fear exploded across the back of his neck as he could feel its presence closing in on him. He was practically jogging, his throat stinging with bile.

He burst through the double doors of the waiting room with a slam, everyone turning to him in surprise. He forced a weak, apologetic smile as he shuffled to the front desk, his ears still ringing.

Kieran gave him a polite, cautious smile. "All done? I can take your packet."

He wordlessly handed it to her, steadying himself on the counter with a white-knuckled grip.

The amber lenses of her bifocals shimmered in the cold, fluorescent light as she quickly gave him a once-over. Her eyes narrowed suspiciously, but she quickly turned her attention back to the packet, skimming through to make sure nothing was missed. "Alright, you should be good to go."

He gave her an acknowledging nod and slipped out the door, patting his pockets for his cigarettes. His heart was still pounding, and his hands shook as he desperately raised his lighter.

The staticky voices and lingering alcohol smell haunted him as he tried to focus on everything around him. The birds, the cars, the wind in the trees. His cigarette smoke, the hot asphalt, the grease from a nearby fast food chain. He steadied himself against the concrete wall, but could feel himself slowly slipping down to his feet. His eyes began to lose focus.

To his right, the door to the adjoining Primary Care burst open and an enthusiastic voice began shouting at him, but it was muffled by the sharp ringing in his ears. A couple quick snaps in his face jarred him from his trance, and he looked up to see Emory, a plastic medical bracelet around their wrist.

His eyes narrowed. He couldn't tell if they were being serious. As he said multiple times in the interview, he never had any idea what was going on in their head. They had a theatrical air about them, and that definitely wasn't something they would say without ulterior motives.

Emory continued, shooting him a smug look. "If you hadn't been an ass, I could've probably resisted it."

Danny held it further away with a squint. It was a news article titled CVRS Opens Investigation of Local Branch Hotspot. The featured photo was the corner store, wrapped in yellow caution tape. He pushed it back towards them with a smirk. "Slow news day?"

"Oh," Danny perked up. "Did you finally ask a VMA for an official copy?"

With a chuffed grin, they unfurled it to reveal that it was, in fact, an updated copy of the Vice Research Compendium, but it was still an agent's copy, their name and ID in the top left corner.

He dragged his hand down his face. "Unbelievable. How did you even have time to steal someone's copy while you were in there? There's something wrong with you."

Emory ignored him. "This new version has tons of neat shit. There's new classifications, and they even introduced subclasses, and there's so much more information about Branches a-and all that stuff! Lots of new stuff about Vice biology, too!" They were enthusiastically jostling the compendium as they rambled on. "And I found out a lot of the notes I'd jotted down as theories in my old copy were actually right! And that's where things get really interesting! I'd done some thinking based on some observations I came across a few months ago. Did you know Vices can make these things called repos that—"

"Emory. Please. Not right now." Orange safety cones draped with caution tape came into view as they approached the store. There was an uneasiness churning deep in his stomach. Emory had done a good job of distracting him, but the budding nausea was getting harder to ignore. His forehead was clammy and his throat, tight. The closer he got to home, the more he wanted to be buried under at least four blankets.

"What do you mean not right now? If you're not gonna apologize, you could at least indulge me and listen to me ramble a bit."

"At the expense of my sanity?"

"Oh, come on. You're always so damn dramatic. Let me gush about repos and we'll call it even. This stuff's super interesting."

"I don't even know what that is, so I can't agree or disagree."

"Cool, I'll tell you about them, then. They're pretty rare, but sometimes when a Vice is mature, it can make a big hulking mass of flesh—"

"No. Hell no, Emory. Not today. Not ever." A pang of nausea urged a rush of heat over his ears. "How about I buy you a beer or something instead? Later, though. I need to go lay down for a bit."

"Or..." Emory trailed off as they skipped over to the cones and playfully gestured to the door. "You could unlock the store for me and I'll leave you alone."

"Why do you want in there? There's nothing to look at! How much could have possibly changed between now and yesterday?"

"I dunno! That's what I wanna find out! If I don't find anything in like 15 minutes, I'll probably get bored and run off anyway, don't worry."

"I take it you see the caution tape but are just electing to ignore it?"

"Like this means anything." They tore a strip from the door and balled it up in their hands. "What is there even to be cautious of, you know?"

"Well, for one, the last time you were in there you had a rogue Branch."

"Oh? Is that a theory I hear?"

"Absolutely not. I'm just saying it doesn't seem terribly smart. And I mean, what if I open the door and the agents are in there? I don't wanna get in trouble, too."

"What, you scared?"

"No, I just can't be bothered. I've had enough CVRS bullshit today."

"Ah, whatever. They're probably not even in there, anyway. I can guarantee you, if there are any agents around, they're taking an absurdly long smoke break in the back alley." Emory squished their face against the tinted glass. "See? The lights are off."

The two nearly jumped out of their shoes as the front door chimed, ripping the caution tape and shoving the safety cones out of the way. Frankie poked her head out with a blank expression.

"You do understand what caution tape means, don't you?" She gestured to the CVRS-branded sign taped to the door. "We even added a sign in case it wasn't clear."

Emory confidently planted their hands on their hips, but their voice was shaky. "Y-yeah, we're just nosy..."

"Glad to see you got out of Primary Care so quickly," Frankie nodded, "but I'm going to have to ask you leave. You, on the other hand," she turned to Danny, "we didn't think to ask for indoor keys. Could you open a few doors for us?"

His shoulders deflated, nausea creeping back up his throat. His pained grimace must have alluded to his reservation.

"I understand it's technically your day off, but it will be quick. We will compensate you for your efforts, as well."

"What!" Emory practically levitated off the ground. "He gets money for unlocking some doors? How's that fair?"

"It's quite fair, actually," she began flatly. "We have commandeered his place of employment; therefore, he will not be paid for today. On top of that, I'm asking him to perform a task for me. The least I could do is compensate him for it."

"God, you have an answer for everything, don't you?"

"Generally." Frankie gestured for Danny to follow her inside, to which he audibly protested, but complied nonetheless.

Emory threw themself at the door as it slammed shut, pressing their entire person against the glass as they watched the two converse.

Danny nervously thumbed through his keys. "Um...just you today?"

"No, Eli is checking the exterior in the back alley." She flipped down her safety glasses and began looking around.

"Oh, okay. Cool." He tried to come off as casually as he could, but his hands were practically shaking as he opened Lucia's office. "Um, the bathrooms should be unlocked, so that just leaves the st—"

Frankie threw out her arm, nearly folding Danny in half. The force from her block urged a very convincing gag that required all of his concentration to suppress. She quickly sent a message with her MobiComm, then pulled a VGMeter from her pocket.

"What's wr—"

"Does this look any different to you?" She pointed to the storage room.

He cocked his head at the off-white door, sitting crooked in its threshold with chipped paint and splintered wood. He pursed his lips. "No? Is something wrong?"

"We cleared this room, correct?"

"You used a...one of those flash bang things, yeah, I remember."

The back door clicked open and Eli jogged into the room, flushed from the summer heat. Even behind his sunglasses, it was hard to hide his delighted surprise. "Oh! Hey, Danny! Everything alright, Frankie?"

She gestured to the storage room. "We cleared this with an AVCharge less than 48 hours ago and look at the state of it. I haven't seen a density like that since the...well, I'm actually not sure I've ever seen a density of that caliber."

He turned around, and his shoulders stiffened. "Oh, wow."

Danny's curiosity was overpowered by his incredibly strong desire to get out of there as quickly as possible. All he had to do was unlock the storage room, and he had fulfilled his duty. His bed was mere minutes away. To him, it was the same storage room that had been there since his first day, the same storage room he had opened dozens of times.

But to Eli and Frankie, it was something else entirely, an anomaly, an unknown. Through their orange lenses, the door shone dazzlingly bright, bleeding a fierce, hot light into the frame around it. The solid white pulsed and throbbed like the rise and fall of steady breath and burned a perfect replica into their retinas.

Eli turned over his shoulder as Danny approached with the key. His calm facade dropped for a moment, fear washing over his pallid face. Dense, white tendrils branched from Danny's stomach, snaking throughout and across his body like a compact, mycelial system.

Danny's hand stopped inches from the door. Sharp ringing pierced through his ears as a dense pressure forced itself against his temples and teased his balance. A thin stream of blood dripped from his nose and infiltrated the corner of his mouth with a pointed, metallic tang. He could feel the floor slipping out from under his feet.

"Danny?" Eli reached for him, his eyes wide in terror.

Frankie whipped her arm behind him as he tumbled backwards, falling against it with a guttural cough that sputtered black muck from his lips.

He lurched forward and stumbled to the ground as sludge was thrust from the depths of his stomach. A pair of hands consoled his trembling shoulders, but felt like knives against his skin. Voices were muffled by the pressure building in his ears. A clot worked its way up his throat and fell from his mouth with a nauseating squelch.

Frankie towered above him as Eli joined him on the floor, rubbing his back and offering feeble attempts at consolation between looking up at her with desperate pleas. Her eyes were locked on Danny, wide and frenzied, because below, through her amber lenses, Danny was heaving viscous globs of liquid light down his chin, an immaculate stream of glittering stardust pooling at his knees.

Shakily, Eli pointed at the clot of tar seeping across the floor tiles. "I-Is that moving?"

Frankie brought her foot down with a powerful stomp, obliterating the writhing pile of sludge into a hissing miasma around her Cleansing Boot. She grabbed Danny by the wrist and whipped him up to his feet as he coughed a dart of black across her cheek.

"W-w-wait, wait, wait!" His voice was garbled by the muck still trapped in his throat. "I'm okay, I'm okay. I just need a minute."

"That is not okay." She pointed sternly at the seeping puddle of sludge beneath them. "I've never seen blights like this, and I've been doing this for 15 years. Let alone a network that dense. How did I not notice this before?"

Danny leaned away and hacked a glob of tar onto the floor, not unlike a cat dislodging a hairball.

"We're taking you to Urgent Care," Frankie stated simply, clicking away at her MobiComm. "Agitated blights of this severity must be addressed immediately."

"Don't really see the point. M'done now," he slurred, dizzied by the pressure in his ears finally equalizing. "S'all good."

Eli sharply whispered behind his hand. "Do it or Frankie will drag you there herself."

"I will not take no for an answer." She turned to him then paused, no longer able to see the dense network of tendrils. Faint wisps of light remained in various areas, likely where the tendrils converged. Eventually it pulsed out of sight, not a trace left behind.

Eli pulled his arm up to his face to inspect it, flipping his sunglasses up and down repeatedly. "Where did it go?"

Frankie hummed to herself. "Omicron subclass. Typically indicative of a Vice with elevated intelligence. All the more reason to get him to Urgent Care."

"I'm fine, it's all good, I promise," Danny insisted. "I've just had a long day. This really isn't out of character for me. I already spent time in Urgent Care for the Branch Recovery interview this morning, and that's probably what did it. That aligns with part of my Branch Ops. Just a delayed reaction. I really don't want to go back."

"Fine. Then you'll go to Primary Care."

He let out a protesting whine, and Eli squeezed his arm, shooting him a whisper. "I know you're stubborn, but you can't out-stubborn Frankie. Just do it."

He relented begrudgingly, tripping over his feet as he headed for the exit. Eli caught up behind him and threw his arm around his shoulder, hoisting him up. His shorter frame struggled with Danny's gangly and unwieldy physique. Frankie loomed behind them, searing her gaze into the back of his neck. Emory was nowhere to be found.

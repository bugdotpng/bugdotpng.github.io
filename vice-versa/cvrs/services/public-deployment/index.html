Public Deployment

Agents in Public Deployment are on-call 24/7, patrolling high-traffic areas in the community, such as streets, shopping hubs, and public events. These agents respond to public and rogue Branches or can be requested to address a potential threat by calling (214) 877-8423. This team also serves as a clean-up crew after Branches have been addressed. Most CVRS stations have multiple Public Deployment teams in rotation to ensure around-the-clock care.

Our Mission

Public Deployment is a relatively new facet of CVRS, established in 2004. As Vices continue to evolve, the way in which they interact with us as a body of Hosts continues to evolve as well. Branches and their impact began as acute, localized instances, but have evolved in both execution and scale, often taking patients by surprise or growing to encompass large swaths of people. Public Deployment agents across the nation are dedicated to detecting and addressing Branches that threaten public safety.

As the threat levels of Vices continue to skew higher and the overall intelligence increases, Public Deployment teams are needed now more than ever to address rogue Branches at risk of affecting large groups of people. Vices, regardless of classification, have begun intentionally initiating Branches in crowded areas, a behavior previously observed exclusively in Theta Vices. This and other new behaviors can be further explored in Vice Biology: Anomalies.

Public Deployment works closely with our Research team to stay on the cutting edge of discoveries. Many observations gathered in the field power discoveries and new understanding as Vices continue to evolve. But Public Deployment agents need their communities’ help in containing these events. Those who manage large-scale events or provide public services should learn about the services we offer below and reach out to the local Public Deployment team to help monitor crowds and keep our community safe.

How to Help

While our agents are working around the clock to maintain public safety, we can’t be everywhere at once or attend all public gatherings. We need the community’s help in prioritizing and being aware of events in advance.

While it is not required, before hosting a large-scale public event (with more than 50 expected attendees), we recommend contacting the local Public Deployment for Event Patrol services in advance.

With Branches, time is of the essence. While our agents do their best to address Branches in a timely manner, already being on the scene can save hours of in-Branch time, especially if the Branch is caught before production. In many cases, being on the scene has been the difference between life and death. Contact the local Public Deployment team today to request our Event Patrol services. It is always better to be safe than sorry.

Beware of Scams

Due to the national Public Deployment agent shortage, grassroots vigilante efforts have begun popping up around the country. While some of these efforts may have good intentions, they are ultimately not affiliated with CVRS and cannot guarantee the precision, technique and care provided by our agents.

Do not accept help from or contract people not affiliated with CVRS. Even former agents should not be contracted, as they are likely to not be up to date on the latest research findings and recommendations.

Always field any requests through our main hotline at (214) 877-8423 or email us at info@cvrs.com. Due to increased traffic, wait times can be longer, so we recommend preparing accordingly and reaching out as far in advance as possible. Additionally, the Intake Coordinator at a local Urgent Care can field any requests in person.

Public Deployment Services

Our agents are on-call 24/7, responding to public Branches or monitoring potential threats at public gatherings. These agents can also dispose of rumina and address post-Branch cleanup to ensure no anomalies have occurred.

Branch Site Cleanup

Performed by Branch Recovery Specialists

Branches, especially suspended Branches, tend to leave behind large quantities of VG after they are addressed, which can lead to increased Branch activity. A Branch Recovery Specialist can be contracted to clean up both public and private Branch sites using our proprietary CVRS technology.

Learn More

Event Patrol

Performed by Target Hunters

Large-scale public events are common Branch initiation spots due to the unpredictable and often overstimulating nature of crowds. Businesses can contract a team of Target Hunters in advance to keep watch and address any potential threats as they arise.

Learn More

Rumen Disposal

Performed by Target Hunters and Branch Administrators

In the event of a located rumen, a Branch Administrator can facilitate both the detachment and destruction of a rumen. These fleshy masses can cause rogue Branches, inexplicable illness and unease, even electrical outages. Site cleanup will also commence after the rumen’s destruction.

Agent Roles

Public Deployment teams are unique in that they are typically comprised of a custom combination of agents to best suit the needs of the local community. For example, areas that often host public events are likely to have multiple Target Hunters on a single team.

While every Public Deployment team is different and not every station has fully staffed teams, the agent roles below all play an important part in protecting our communities. Click on a role to learn more about their area of expertise and the training they received.

  • Branch Administrator
  • Branch Coordinator
  • Solutions Architect
  • Branch Recovery Specialist
  • Target Hunter

Other CVRS Services

Primary Care

A station’s Primary Care wing is open by appointment only on weekdays 7:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Primary Care provides long-term treatment for patients with an active Vice and are admitted by a Record Proctor. Treatment can last anywhere from a few months or be lifelong, but know that our dedicated agents will be with our patients every step of the way.

Learn More

Urgent Care

A station’s Urgent Care wing is open 24/7. If a Branch has been suspended in the last 12 hours, we recommend admitting for Branch remediation services, while recently committed Branches may require our Branch recovery services. Patients can also be admitted for activity monitoring if they are at risk of an impending Branch initiation. Urgent Care also treats at-risk patients referred from Primary Care and Public Deployment.

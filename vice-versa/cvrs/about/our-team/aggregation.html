Aggregation

The Aggregation team primarily curates, sorts, and prioritizes information submitted by the Observation team and agents from Public Health & Safety. These agents parse large amounts of data to find patterns and changes in Vice behavior and biology.

Aggregation Agents

While every Aggregation team is different, most teams have a single Lead Station Aggregator, two Forecast Specialists and multiple Report Coordinators. The agent roles below all play an important part in organizing and enhancing the local and national research databases. Click on a role to learn more about their area of expertise and the training they received.

  • Lead Station Aggregator
  • Forecast Specialist

Lead Station Aggregator R01-100

The mouthpiece of the Research sector, these agents compile and present findings to the executive branch to potentially be included in the master CVRS database. As with other aggregators, these agents do not have face time with patients and deal exclusively in objective data. They discern whether anomalies are acute or indicative of larger patterns and present findings to the executive research board quarterly.

These agents are excellent public speakers and efficient communicators and are expected to be intimately familiar with both the local and master research database.

Lead Station Aggregators must complete Basic Training and are not required to have a patch.

Forecast Specialist R02-100

Forecast Specialists parse and extrapolate the data aggregated by Report Coordinators. These agents look for trends in Vice behavior and monitor potential evolutionary changes. These trends are compiled and submitted to the local CVRS database to help guide treatment plans in Primary Care and Urgent Care. Forecast Specialists also have a close relationship with Target Hunters, who rely on their observations and predictions to better survey public spaces.

Forecast Specialists must complete Basic Training and are not required to have a patch.

Report Coordinator R03-100

Report Coordinators are true aggregators, with their main focus being to organize and cull the wealth of data from agents across all CVRS teams to add to the local CVRS database. These agents deal with any and all observations submitted by agents using their MobiComm, whether that be Primary Care agents documenting a patient’s response to treatment, a Vice Analysts’ observations of an active Branch as a pseudo Adjunct, or a Branch Administrator’s log of Vice behavior inside the Branch Arena.

Case-specific observations and reports are summarized and added to their respective case file for easy review, often with references back to established data trends in the database. Report Coordinators are relatively isolated in their interactions with both agents and patients to maintain objectivity in their data aggregation.

Forecast Specialists must complete Basic Training and are not required to have a patch.

Observation

Observation is a unique team of pseudo Adjuncts who accompany both the Primary and Urgent Care teams in Branches of all types, from standard to rogue to instigated. These agents do not interfere with or have an impact on active Branches and send their observations to Aggregation for review.

Manufacturing

This specialized team of designers and engineers are responsible for the gear and technology that optimize agent productivity. From the iconic Parsing Goggles to the innovative Bypasses, this team is always researching ways to make the jobs of agents easier.

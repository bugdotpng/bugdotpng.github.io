Careers

Interested in pursuing a career with CVRS? If you’re passionate about community impact and thrive in a fast-paced environment, you’ll fit right in. Learn how to apply and get an overview of what will be expected of you as an agent.

Contact Us

For more information, call us at (214) 877-8423 or send an email to info@cvrs.com.

The field of vicology has been rapidly growing since its inception in 1973, and we need bright, passionate agents now more than ever. As Vices continue to evolve, so do our strategies and tactics, and we need support not only on the frontline, but behind the scenes as researchers and administrators as well.

We take pride in our agents, but we also take care of them. Our state-of-the-art stations ensure that every agent has what they need to succeed, whether that’s equipment, training, or support. Join our family today!

Ready to Apply?

We’re excited to meet you! We have provided multiple ways to apply to best fit your needs. Find us on LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor to discover open positions in your area.

Alternatively, you can email us at info@cvrs.com with your CV, resume, and references to express interest in your desired area and we’ll forward your application to the appropriate station number.

Primary Care wings will also accept in-person applications for all positions, no matter the team.

Agent Training

As an agent, you will need to complete different degrees of training depending on your role in order to be onboarded. After certification, agents are required to stay up to date with the ever-growing field of Vices and must complete supplementary training courses every two years.

Agent Gear

Depending on your role, you will be supplied with state-of-the-art gear designed to aid in your treatment or research duties. From tablets to advanced Vice combat technology, agents have everything they need to succeed in their career.

Knowledge is Power

Before you pursue a career, we recommend familiarizing yourself with all the career options available at CVRS. Many of our agents have interdisciplinary skill sets, and you may discover a role previously unknown that sparks your interest.

PHS

Primary Care

Primary Care agents provide long-term treatment for patients who have been screened and admitted by a Record Administrator. These agents are equipped with the knowledge and training to help patients along their Vice journey and even perform rescission when appropriate.

Learn More
PHS

Urgent Care

Agents in Urgent Care provide acute care and treat at-risk patients from both Primary Care and Public Deployment, as well as public admissions. Urgent Care is trained to commit threatening Branches and refer patients back to Primary Care to resume or begin treatment.

Learn More
PHS

Public Deployment

Agents in Public Deployment are on-call 24/7, often patrolling high-traffic hot spots, led by their resident Target Hunter. These agents respond to public Branches or can be requested to monitor a potential threat. These agents also address post-Branch cleanup to ensure no anomalies have occurred.

Learn More
Research

Aggregation

The Aggregation team primarily curates, sorts, and prioritizes information submitted by the Observation team and agents from Public Health & Safety who are in active Branches. These agents parse large amounts of data to find patterns and changes in Vice behavior and biology.

Learn More
Research

Observation

Observation is a unique team of pseudo Adjuncts who accompany both the Primary and Urgent Care teams in Branches of all types, from standard to rogue to instigated. These agents do not interfere with or have an impact on active Branches and send their observations to Aggregation for review.

Learn More
Research

Manufacturing

This specialized team of designers and engineers are responsible for the gear and technology that optimize agent productivity. From the iconic Parsing Goggles to the innovative Bypasses, this team is always researching ways to make the jobs of agents easier.

Learn More
Support

Sector Advocates

Integral to agents’ success, both sectors have a Sector Advocate. These agents do not engage with patients or facilitate research; instead, they perform the administrative duties for the agents of their respective sector, including hiring, employee relations, and funding requests.

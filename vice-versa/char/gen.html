Agent Profile — A14706239

Vice Management Specialist, Primary Care

Genesis Schwarzschild has been an agent with CVRS for 6 years, currently performing the role of a Vice Management Specialist for Station 147 Primary Care in the Central Texas Wing. In addition to these duties, she also works closely with the Station 147 Research Sector to spearhead case studies focused on Vice intelligence. Gen is a diligent and fastidious agent who is attentive and successful in facilitating rescission for the large majority of her patients.

Advent

Over the course of roughly three months, the patient experienced a gradual advent at age 14. Various emotions and circumstances contributed to advent, ranging from the stress of starting high school to the pressure to perform at an outrageously high level. The patient had a strained relationship with her parents—who the majority of the pressure stemmed from—and was also stressed about the well-being of a close friend. The multitude of emotions present during this gradual advent lent itself well to the Epsilon classification, though it was misclassified as Beta upon intake due to its gradual advent and persistent blights. The Vice core was verified with a VGDS after the patient was admitted for blight manifestation.

Patch Notes

Patient's patch was summoned after B1472730401-04, particularly after a nasty bout of post-Branch blights. Patch formation was facilitated and expedited by her Vice Management Specialist, as she recognized early on the severity of her Vice. It resembles the scythes that the patient gravitated toward in video games and animated media, not modeled after a specific instance, but rather an amalgamation of inspiration from various sources. Patient has positive feelings toward her patch and feels that it represents and serves her well.

Efficacy

The patient’s patch is well-suited to the exhibition, its large blade able to inflict massive damage on the exhibition’s frail form. The difficulty in damaging lies in the exhibition’s ability to fly, though the scythe’s long metal staff serves the patient well in performing ranged attacks. Inspired by her secondary Adjunct, Emory Torres, when wielded around the lower handle and with enough momentum, it can be used as a thrown weapon to hit the exhibition when airborne.

Branch Formula

Because of the Vice’s classification, Branch Formula changed often in the Vice’s life cycle, acting on different triggers depending upon which gave it the greatest advantage at the time. Triggers were primarily phrases similar to those said around the time of advent, though sometimes textures and specific foods triggered Branches as well, drawing from later stressors. Phrases included The drastic change in Branch formula and Branch Arena of B1472730401-05 earned a reclassification from Beta to Epsilon. Following B1472730401-12, the Vice received the additional subclass of --gamma after the patient’s Vice Management Specialist observed the thoughtful curation and facilitation of Branches.

Changes

The exhibition shifted five times in the Vice’s life cycle, though it always maintained its capacity for flight. This led the Vice Management Specialist and Solutions Architects to infer that the core placement was in or around its wings, likely at the base, as proved to be the case.

Branch Arenas tended to change quite drastically compared to its exhibition, focusing on recreating pixel-perfect renditions of scenery where the patient had experienced strong negative emotions in the past, examples including the alley behind her middle school, the kitchen in her childhood home, and the living room of her middle school violin teacher. While each change in the Branch Arena was relatively drastic, the height of the arena grew with each shift.

Branch History

Below is the patient's recorded Branch history, listed in reverse chronological order. The patient has completed rescission and is now cleared.

June 06, 2012 • Instigated

B1472730401-33 | Rescission Attempt Rescinded

  • Phases: Initiation, Staging, Production
  • Duration: 00:04:57C, 00:45:28M
  • Extensions:
    • Host: Genesis Schwarzschild (patch: scythe)
    • Primary Adjunct: Mitsuki Yoshida, Vice Management Specialist (patch: crossbow)
    • Primary Adjunct: Rami Hakim (patch: dual handaxes)
    • Secondary Adjunct: Darrin Edwards, Branch Admin (patch: broadsword)
    • Secondary Adjunct: Craig Waters, Vice Management Coordinator (patch: throwing knives)
    • Pseudo Adjunct: Gina Hahn, Solutions Architect

The Branch was instigated in the observation chambers by the Branch Proctor. It was successfully instigated using a well-established track, and all Adjuncts were successfully queued. Due to instigation, staging was quite short compared to past staging times, at roughly 00:00:25C, 00:03:00M.

Upon entry, it was noted that the Branch Arena had changed drastically between productions. Where the previous Branch Arena was expansive and detailed, this Branch Arena was quite cramped, but extremely tall. The exhibition was hidden upon the start of production, presumed to be hovering toward the top of the arena, where it planned to simply stall the Branch as it pursued germination. Solutions Architect Gina Hahn verified this to be true, able to observe the arena from a wider point of view.

Branch Coordinator Craig Waters and Primary Adjunct Rami Hakim were able to use their throwing patches to injure the exhibition and force it to fall. As the exhibition came into view, it was noted that its lower body had been altered considerably: its legs were stronger, its talons were much larger, curved, and sharper, and a large, spiked tail was used as a whip-like weapon, wherein the exhibition had no tail before.

Immediately recognizing this threat, Gen was able to briefly utilize her scythe as a throwing weapon to sever the exhibition's lower body. Vice Management Specialist Mitsuki Yoshida rendered the exhibition's wings unusable with the use of her crossbow, causing it to finally fall, unable to move. Branch Admin Darrin Edwards delivered the final blow by driving his broadsword into the base of the exhibition's wing to destroy its core.

June 02, 2012 • Rogue

B1472730401-32 Suspended

  • Phases: Initiation, Staging
  • Duration: ~00:00:45C, 00:00:00M
  • Extensions: none

Initiation occurred during the patient's panic attack, of which she was having late in the evening at her partner and primary Adjunct Rami's house. Rami recalls the subject of the panic attack being along the lines of an existential crisis, a fear that her body of work had been a waste of time. He was able to recognize the warning signs of a rogue Branch (agitated bights and blown pupils, particularly) and felt the pull of staging shortly after.

Rami administered an AVShot he had on hand to perform suspension and terminate the Branch. The patient lost consciousness due to suspension. Rami admitted her to Urgent Care shortly after for monitoring. He stayed with the patient overnight and attended her Pre-Rescission Assessment the following day.
